Our Background


Grace on heart charity foundation is a charity non-profit organization founded in Uganda in 2015 but fully registered in 2019. The vision of GOHC charity foundation is to create a sustainable healthy, literate and self- reliant, generation in Uganda and Africa at large. We believe that this will bring positive change to the lives of the challenged potential and school-going children and youth that are at risk of missing education as a basic necessity in Uganda and Africa at large. The organization works to improve the lives of school-going children especially orphans and their communities in Uganda, through education, out riches, healthy, and sanitation as well as skills development.


We believe in building a brighter future by investing in the children, the people, and the communities that they live in. What we do as an organization committed to helping children better themselves; cannot be done without the help of like you. Grace on heart charity foundation doesn’t necessarily work on its own, we believe everyone has something to give; it can be your time, your knowledge and expertise, or a financial donation. We believe that success due to the implementation of these programs will come only if we pattern with individuals, communities and other mindful organizations within our line of operation.

Mission To Africa


Grace on heart charity exists to build a reputable movement with a long term commitment to promote and monitor the fulfillment of the right to basic and secular education.

As part of its program to carry out that objective, GOHC works with similar individuals and groups to exchange information, time, resources and talents to build the capacity of our members. Therefore, we are proud to offer you a unique service experience in the form of Africa Mission Trips. These mission trips give team members the opportunity to serve individuals particularly children, and families in Uganda through the sharing of time and talents.

No act of compassion, love, and service is too small to make a difference in the lives of those that live in wait of hope that perhaps tomorrow will be a delight. It is our assurance to anyone that participates in these mission trips that, “Your impact will be felt for years to come!”

When you don’t have a lot of money, putting food on the table can be tough. So what do families do in places such as Uganda where resources are scarce and money is scarcer? Between 2013 and 2015, it is estimated that more than 500,000 young Ugandan children died. Of deaths, nearly half were associated with under nutrition. Under nutrition is responsible for four in 10 deaths of children under five.

The 2012 study, Cost of Hunger in Uganda, estimated the health cost of children’s under nutrition-related illnesses (for those under five) to be more than UGX 525 billion, most of which was used for treating under nutrition and associated illnesses. It is also estimated that under nutrition costs Uganda 1.8 trillion UGX, an equivalent of 5.6 per cent of its GDP annually.
